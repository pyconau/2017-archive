Information Pack for Special Events @ PyCon AU 2016

PyCon Australia 2016 is being held in Melbourne from Friday August 12 to Tuesday August 16. The first day of the conference is the Specialist Day.

In previous years, special events associated with PyCon Australia have been organised by invitation of the main conference organising committee. This year, an open call will be issued so that any group wishing to organise a special event may apply (including to organisers of special events in previous years). The organising committee will then review and select the events to include in the PyCon Australia 2016 program. Special event organisers will then be brought on as an event organising team, working with the main organising committee and other special event organisers to put together a great community driven event.

Examples of special events that could be included in the program:

  • Up to 4 specialist tracks (aka "miniconfs") for Friday's Specialist Day (e.g. DjangoCon AU, Python in Education seminar)
  • Workshops running in parallel with the development sprints (e.g. DjangoGirls, Young Coders)
  • Associated events run independently of the conference, but taking advantage of the presence of a large contingent of Pythonistas in Melbourne (e.g. Software Carpentry workshops)
  • Icebreaker events for Friday evening or Social events during the sprints

These events are all hailed as a major highlight by conference attendees and rely on community members coming forward to help organise them. It’s also our chance to help out these groups, as  organisers can take advantage of the gathered forces of the Australian Python community without the full burden of organising a national event.

This is our call to the community: please put up your hand and make yourself known. The 2016 organising committee would like to continue to support these activities while moving towards a more transparent and structured process. This document outlines the process by which special events may be organised.

The following aspects of the event are part of the main conference program and will be handled directly by the conference organising committee:

  • Weekend talk and tutorial program
  • Logistics for Friday's Specialist Day on-site events
  • Call for papers (including for specialist tracks)
  • Conference dinner (Saturday)
  • Development sprints (Monday/Tuesday, introduction Sunday)
  • Financial aid program

We are asking all those interested in running either a workshop or a full specialist day track to register their interest with the PyCon AU organising committee. It is early in the process so there is a chance to inform the committee around how to best support these events. If you are interested in running a workshop, seminar, miniconf or outreach activity, please let us know.

Note that the Friday specialist tracks are logistically integrated with the main conference (sharing the venue, ticketing, call for papers, etc), but track organisers are responsible for working with the main program committee to craft a compelling talk and tutorial schedule that targets specialist audiences on the Friday, and a more general developer and community audience on the weekend.

In previous years, some special events have been organised late in the piece. This schedule is a nominal time-frame to encourage people to engage earlier, however the committee will consider late submissions if they will improve the conference. Early submissions will have priority for rooms and other support.

Support provided by PyCon AU organising committee

  • Insurance and liability coverage for events held at the conference venue
  • Access to email channels and other marketing efforts
  • The main conference call for presentations will also cover Specialist Day events
  • If relevant, can organise additional rooms at the venue at standard prices (see FAQ)
  • Support for funding grant applications to Linux Australia, the PSF or other organisations
  • Morning tea, lunch and afternoon tea provided at the conference venue for those who have purchased a ticket to the Specialist Day (on-site events only)
  • Organise registration of attendees

Responsibilities of a Special Event team (i.e. you)

  • Organise any funding required, including the cost of the venue if needed (see FAQ)
  • Maintain communication with the PyCon AU organising committee when planning
  • Organise any staff and volunteers required to run the event
  • Organise logistics on the day and run the event

How Special Events will be Selected

The selection itself will be handled through a special meeting of the PyCon AU organising committee, coordinated through the programme chair, as follows:

  • Benefits to the PyCon AU conference experience
  • Benefits to the Python community
  • Benefits to particular community groups or areas
  • Readiness and ability of the working group to deliver
  • Quality of the submission documentation

Frequently Asked Questions:

This section will be updated frequently

Q: I’m interested in running an event, what do I do next?

A: Email the organising committee at contact@pycon-au.org to register your interest as soon as possible, just let us know who you are and what you might be interested in running. Then, take the next 4 weeks to develop your expression of interest according to the template below.

Q: What kind of event can I run?

A: We are encouraging people to be creative. It is perfectly okay to target a specific audience with a restricted demographics. This could be due to the niche nature of an event (pypy.js) or due to the domain being discussed (education, data science). The event is ticketed separately, so your attendees don’t need to pay for the full conference to attend. That said, we would also be looking for some events which will appeal to those looking to extend their two-day conference. Based on the last couple of years attendance, the specialist day attendance is rapidly growing to the same level of significance at the main event, which is a great achievement.

Q: Can I organise my own sponsors if I want to?

A: This may be an option. You will need to do this in consultation with the PyCon AU organising committee to ensure this is handled effectively. Sponsorship opportunities will be assessed on a case-by-case basis by the organising committee; if your event will need funding, please make sure that this is noted in your proposal.

Q: How will the call for proposals be organised this year?

A: This is still to be finalised and more information will be shared soon. The organising committee are still actively working to deliver an improved and streamlined process that provides greater clarity.

Q: How is this process being managed?

A: The programme chair (Tennessee Leeuwenburg) is coordinating the process. This early in proceedings, it is possible that elements of the procedure may change. The new process aims to provide greater clarity, transparency and integrity to the process. We ask those involved to provide their feedback, but also for their understanding that this is a volunteer exercise and that as such we need to rely on a collaborative approach.

Q: How are A/V requirements or other special needs for special events handled?

A: The four miniconf-style full-day tracks on the Friday are usually recorded as per any other day of the conference. Events not on-site will need to handle their own AV requirements. If you have other needs for your event, please get in touch to discuss what those might be.

Q: How does room hire work?

A (for full-day on-site tracks): We have accounted for four miniconfs being run on Friday, with room hire to be handled by the PyCon AU organising committee. Those selected events will not need to organise their own funding, as that will be handled through the conference ticketing process. Should the organising committee select additional events that would require additional space, funding for those will be handled on a case-by-case basis.

A: (off-site and additional rooms): Once we have exceeded the on-site room booking we have planned for, there may be additional costs. There is a small/medium additional room available on-site at additional cost, otherwise your event will need to run off-site. Your event will need to organise to bear those costs, but get in touch with the organising committee to discuss that. Options include additional sponsorship, an additional cost, or anything you can come up with.

Q: There is not enough information here! My question isn’t answered! Something’s wrong!

A: We understand. At this stage, the focus is on registering your interest rather than on detailed planning and implementation. We will be updating this document as those questions come in and letting everyone know if any changes or a key decision is made. Organising large events is pretty difficult, so be patient and we will sort everything out.
